Spaghetti Carbonara

A classic Italian pasta dish with a creamy cheese sauce and crispy pancetta.

Easy

Preparation Time:

30 minutes

Servings:

2

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil a large pot of salted water. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat a pan over medium heat. Fry the pancetta until crisp and golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs and grated Parmesan cheese.
  4. Reserve 1/2 cup of the pasta water before draining the pasta. Drain the pasta and return it to the pot.
  5. Immediately add the pancetta, garlic, and reserved pasta water to the pot with the pasta. Toss to combine.
  6. Remove the pan from heat and slowly add the egg mixture, stirring constantly, until a creamy sauce forms.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve immediately with additional grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.

Tips

  • For a richer flavor, use freshly grated Parmesan cheese.
  • You can substitute bacon for the pancetta.
  • Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.

Nutritional Information

Per Serving (approximate):

  • Calories: 450
  • Fat: 25g
  • Carbohydrates: 35g
  • Protein: 20g
